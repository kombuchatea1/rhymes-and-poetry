A bad dream is sometimes a small mercy. It shows you the shape of a fear you'd been carrying without knowing. This is from one of those nights.

<div class="poem-block">

I woke up last night, drenched in sweat<br>
I had a horrible dream<br>
All my worst fears realized: a life of regret<br>
Everything happened at once but now I forget<br>
I just remember how I felt, but not what it means

Was this a premonition?<br>
Or a wake up call for healing?<br>
To change what may have already been written<br>
Dreams are often our mind's fiction<br>
A mere play of our souls' deepest feelings

I wonder why that dream, and why now<br>
Was it supposed to happen?<br>
Or did I manifest it somehow?<br>
Either way I'll take a vow<br>
I'll devote myself to positivity and laughing

I've made it this far in life, thankfully<br>
And if this resonates, know that you can too<br>
A bad dream is not your reality<br>
Just unpleasant gravity<br>
Spin those thoughts and learn to live anew

</div>

A bad dream is not your reality — it's just unpleasant gravity. The thing that woke you up gets to be a warning, not a sentence. What you do in the morning is the part that matters.

<div class="signature">

— JTC
