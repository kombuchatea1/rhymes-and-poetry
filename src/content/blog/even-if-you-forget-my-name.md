A long one. About what gets to count, and the version of legacy worth working toward.

<div class="poem-block">

I won't know 'till it's all said and done<br>
If I can be proud of the man I've become<br>
Dangerous times and delicate minds<br>
Destroys a sense of pride; we're lost in time

Days are weeks and weeks are months<br>
Months are years, and I feel<br>
That I don't dream like I used to<br>
What mattered then, won't matter now

And what matters now won't matter<br>
All I can do, all we can do is go<br>
With the wind, go toward what beckons<br>
Our souls; intuition: I've always listened

I trust myself, but I don't trust others<br>
Yet I know a good heart when I feel one<br>
I don't want to become jaded<br>
I stay in my lane, and hope beyond hope

That I've made a difference<br>
That you felt an impact<br>
And that love will remain<br>
Even if you forget my name

</div>

Most legacies aren't names on plaques. They're the way someone you'd forgotten about still talks to themselves a certain way, because of something you said, because of something you did. *Even if you forget my name* — the love still travels.

<div class="signature">

— JTC

</div>
