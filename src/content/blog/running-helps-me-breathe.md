There's a thing the body does, on the right run, that nothing else can do for me. This poem is about that.

<div class="poem-block">

Running helps me breathe<br>
And helps me achieve<br>
Things I could only dream<br>
Like having self-esteem

And having a body I'm proud of<br>
I've had a hard time with self-love<br>
But running is always there<br>
You can bring it with you anywhere

Sometimes I forget I have that light<br>
When I've slowed down, in bed at night<br>
With thoughts of feeling low<br>
Desperate to let them go

Yet once I start my runs<br>
Feelings of bliss becomes<br>
Part of my heart, part of my soul<br>
Running is simply how I take control

</div>

Running won't fix everything. But on the days when nothing in my head is cooperating, my legs usually are. Sometimes that's the door back in.

<div class="signature">

— JTC
