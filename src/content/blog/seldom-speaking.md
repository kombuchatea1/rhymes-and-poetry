A harder one. About the noise we're swimming in and the fact that almost none of it qualifies as actual conversation.

<div class="poem-block">

We're connected<br>
But never present<br>
We're always affected<br>
But often complacent

It feels like being sad<br>
after sex<br>
As if relief comes only<br>
from death<br>
For there are sinners<br>
saving lives<br>
And saints who are selfish<br>
and unkind

What is this world?<br>
It feels upside down<br>
What is this place<br>
I once called home?

Everyone is yelling —<br>
seldom speaking<br>
And no one dares to sit<br>
and listen<br>
With pride, everyone says<br>
they're angry<br>
But I don't think they<br>
even know why

</div>

The hardest version of the truth right now: most people aren't actually angry about the thing they're loud about. The thing they're loud about is just a vehicle for the underlying ache. The poem doesn't try to fix it — it just names what most of us suspect on the quiet days.

<div class="signature">

— JTC
