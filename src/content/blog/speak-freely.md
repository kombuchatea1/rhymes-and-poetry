For the people we lost without finishing what we wanted to say. And for everyone we still get the chance to.

<div class="poem-block">

If I had known it'd be the last time<br>
Maybe I could've said more<br>
So you knew how I felt; no doubts<br>
Why hadn't I expressed this before?

If we could have just one more talk<br>
Oh the things I could have learned<br>
You were the embodiment of wisdom<br>
Perhaps discipline is what I've earned

Now I want to make you proud<br>
Because you always believed<br>
When I didn't believe in myself<br>
You lived a life many wish they could achieve

We're often too naive<br>
Too wrapped up in our lives<br>
To realize our time together's short<br>
So speak freely, and from the heart

</div>

The lesson always arrives after the test. The poem ends with the only practical takeaway: *speak freely, and from the heart.* Not later. Not when it's safer. Now.

<div class="signature">

— JTC
