A long one. Sometimes the best way to talk about a thing is to talk about something else that has the same shape.

<div class="poem-block">

Imagine yourself a pilot<br>
Flying through the sky<br>
It's only you up there<br>
The path's for you to decide

You see clear blue skies<br>
Sunshine beaming through<br>
You can't imagine a more perfect day<br>
It doesn't matter where you're headed to

But eventually, there's a flurry of dark clouds<br>
A storm is approaching, onward<br>
You can't stop, you can't turn around<br>
You can only move forward

You panic, your heart races faster than ever before<br>
Truly frightened, because this could be it<br>
But you keep going — because you don't have a choice<br>
Suddenly, the clouds disperse, and you're back in paradise

</div>

You can't stop the storms. You can't outrun them either. The only direction available is *through.* And the strange grace of the metaphor is that the storms always end — eventually, the clouds disperse, and the sky is back.

Keep flying.

<div class="signature">

— JTC
