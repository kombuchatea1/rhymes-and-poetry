A long one. Asks the questions you usually save for milestone birthdays.

<div class="poem-block">

Like candles in the dark<br>
Like sands in an hourglass<br>
I push myself to embark<br>
On a journey to surpass

What I was the day before<br>
What I was a lifetime ago<br>
Yet I rest and ignore<br>
The fire inside; desperate to grow

Instead I gaze at the sea, and sin<br>
A constant wonder of what could be<br>
Or what could have been<br>
Wishing I knew the key for me

To feel as though it wasn't all for naught<br>
To feel as though I've made the most<br>
Of precious moments I have caught<br>
And know that I was truly engrossed

Before too long that candle will lose its light<br>
And before too long, the sand will cease<br>
Did I use it well? Did I use it with all my might?<br>
Or did I let it decay? Will I ever know peace?

It's hard to say while the candle still burns<br>
And it's hard to see if I've used time effectively<br>
I just feel I haven't done enough to earn<br>
A life of happiness, of serenity; truly being free

</div>

You don't get to know if you used the time well until you don't have any left. That's a bad joke and a true one. The honest answer the poem lands on isn't comforting — it's the *not knowing.* The work is doing the best you can without the answer.

<div class="signature">

— JTC

</div>
