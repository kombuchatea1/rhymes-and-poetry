# Content pillars

R&P has five content categories — matching the topic enum in `src/content.config.ts` and the canonical definitions in `src/consts/topics.ts`.

## reflections (the largest pillar)

Personal observations on growth, change, attention, mortality, identity, time. The bulk of the catalog. Examples:

- "I Wish I Could See" — late-night questions about meaning
- "Live Presently" — three-line presence haiku
- "Some Days" — variability of mood
- "Time Is a Vessel" — time as gift and thief

## love-loss

Romance, heartbreak, friendship, parental love, grief, soulmate energy. Examples:

- "Until I Met You" — discovery of love
- "Frozen in Time" — outgrown people
- "To My Dear Friend" — letter to someone gone
- "Oh Oliver" — the dog

## nature

The natural world as mirror — oceans, trees, rain, seasons. Examples:

- "Pull of the Universe" — waves and destiny
- "Walking by the Sea" — solitude and connection
- "The Sight of Trees" — quiet forest clarity
- "Distant Lands" — travel as nourishment

## community

Pieces about other people, society, the shared human condition. Examples:

- "We're Not So Different" — strangers and recognition
- "I Had a Good Day" — parallel emotional weather
- "Two Complete Strangers" — brief eye contact
- "Age of Information" — modern noise

## rap-and-bars

Short rhyme-driven pieces with cadence and bars. Intentionally a small minority of the catalog — R&P is no longer rap-focused. Examples:

- "Spreading Peace" — six bars
- "Symbolic Bars" — self-deprecating
- "Forms Like a Nuke Bomb" — five bars on cadence

## How to assign category for new posts

Each poem gets exactly one category. Pick based on the poem's primary emotional/thematic register, not its incidental imagery. A poem with the word "ocean" isn't automatically nature if it's really about love.

If unsure: lean toward `reflections` (the catch-all for introspective work). Use `love-loss` for anything aimed at a specific person. Use `community` for anything observing others or society.
