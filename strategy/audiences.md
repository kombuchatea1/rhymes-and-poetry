# Audiences

## Primary: readers who connect with reflective writing

People in their 20s–40s who:

- Save poems they relate to
- Send screenshots to friends instead of articles
- Don't necessarily think of themselves as "poetry readers" but recognize honesty when they see it
- Have followed JTC on Instagram OR found R&P via someone sharing a piece

This audience reads piece-by-piece, not in long sessions. The site needs to be skim-friendly, with the painterly hero image doing emotional lift and the prose framing giving each piece weight.

## Secondary: long-term newsletter subscribers

People who liked enough pieces to want them in their inbox. Twice a month, no padding. This list is the long-term asset — algorithm-proof, fully owned, and the foundation for any future paid offering.

## Tertiary (eventual): custom-poem clients

People who need a poem for a wedding, anniversary, funeral, milestone. They'll pay for craft and speed. Not a primary acquisition channel — these come from readers who already trust the brand.

## Who R&P is NOT trying to reach

- "Poetry tools" search traffic (people looking for rhyme finders, caption generators)
- Mass-market motivational-poetry consumers
- The academic or literary establishment

## How that shapes the site

- Hero images are emotional, painterly, atmospheric — not stock or didactic
- The journal index ("Every poem, in order") is the primary discovery surface, not a search bar
- The newsletter ask is framed as "stay close to the words," not "subscribe for offers"
- About page is personal narrative, not credentials or "what I sell"
