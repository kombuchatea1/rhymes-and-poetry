# Monetization roadmap

## Phase 0 (now) — build trust, no monetization

Goal is brand depth, voice consistency, traffic foundation. Newsletter is the only ask, framed as "stay close to the words," not "subscribe for offers."

Success metrics for moving to Phase 1:

- Newsletter at 500+ subscribers, OR
- Site at ~5k unique visitors/month consistently

## Phase 1 (3–6 months out) — custom poems

Open custom poem orders. Suggested pricing:

- Short poem for a moment (text / card length): **$40–60**
- Wedding / anniversary / memorial piece: **$150–300**
- Speech polishing or vow writing: **$200–400**

Quiet launch — a single page on the site, mentioned in newsletter occasionally, no aggressive push. Audience trust matters more than conversion rate at this stage.

## Phase 2 (6–12 months) — small digital products

If newsletter is healthy:

- A printed book or zine of selected pieces (print-on-demand via Lulu or BookBaby)
- A "writing prompts from the journal" PDF — possibly free as a newsletter signup incentive

## Phase 3 (longer term) — community / membership

Only if there's clear demand from existing readers. Patreon, Substack, or similar — small private monthly drop, behind-the-scenes notes, work-in-progress pieces. Not a priority unless the foundation is strong.

## What we won't do

- Sponsored posts that don't fit the brand
- Display ads on the site
- Affiliate links on grief / loss pages
- AI-generated content sold as Jake's work
- Membership tiers that gate the existing journal

The brand is built on honesty. The monetization has to extend from that, not contradict it.
