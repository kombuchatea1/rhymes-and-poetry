# Rhymes & Poetry Site Rebuild + AI Agent Growth Playbook

**Site:** rhymesandpoetry.com  
**Primary goal:** rebuild the site into a poetry/creative-writing brand that grows search traffic, captures email subscribers, routes visitors to Instagram, and monetizes through digital products, affiliates, services, sponsorships, and AI-assisted content workflows.  
**Secondary goal:** implement practical AI agents that reduce manual work without turning the brand into generic AI content.

---

## 1. North Star Strategy

Rhymes & Poetry should become a destination for people who want help with emotional writing, rhymes, poems, captions, vows, lyrics, creative prompts, and personalized expression.

The brand should not compete as “another poetry blog.” It should become a **utility + inspiration platform**:

- **Utility:** rhyme help, poem templates, writing prompts, Instagram captions, wedding speech help, apology/love/birthday poem generators, SEO-focused guides.
- **Inspiration:** original poems, short-form poetic posts, reels, visual quote cards, emotional storytelling.
- **Monetization:** templates, paid custom poems, downloadable packs, affiliate recommendations, sponsored tools/products, newsletter, future memberships.
- **Distribution:** Google search, AI search visibility, Instagram, Pinterest, email, and short-form video.

The best positioning:

> **Rhymes & Poetry helps people find the right words for the moments that matter.**

That phrase should inform homepage copy, content, Instagram bio, lead magnets, and product ideas.

## 4. SEO Strategy

Google’s public guidance continues to emphasize helpful, reliable, people-first content, and Google’s AI search documentation says site owners should focus on content quality, crawlability, and standard search controls rather than trying to manipulate AI systems. Build for humans first, but structure content so search engines and AI systems can understand it.

### SEO Principles

- Each page should solve a specific user intent.
- Avoid thin “AI-generated” pages with generic poems.
- Add original examples, personal commentary, emotional nuance, and practical guidance.
- Use clear headings, FAQ sections, internal links, schema, and short introductions.
- Build topic clusters instead of random posts.
- Include original writing samples that are not found elsewhere.
- Create pages that are useful even if the reader never clicks another page.

## 6. AI Agent System

The goal is not to let AI replace the brand voice. The goal is to use AI agents to research, draft, format, repurpose, and optimize content while you keep creative control.

### Agent 1: SEO Research Agent

**Purpose:** identify keyword opportunities and content gaps.

**Inputs:** topic, target audience, competitor URLs, Search Console data, Google Trends, keyword data.  
**Outputs:** keyword list, article briefs, search intent notes, title ideas, internal-link suggestions.

**Prompt template:**

```text
You are the SEO Research Agent for Rhymes & Poetry.

Goal: Find high-intent content opportunities for people searching for poems, rhymes, captions, vows, lyrics, and emotional writing help.

For the topic below, produce:
1. Primary keyword
2. Secondary keywords
3. Search intent
4. Suggested H1
5. Suggested H2 outline
6. FAQ questions
7. Internal link opportunities
8. Monetization CTA
9. Instagram repurposing idea

Topic: {{TOPIC}}
Brand voice: emotionally intelligent, clear, poetic but not cheesy, useful, human.
```

### Agent 2: Content Brief Agent

**Purpose:** turn keyword opportunities into article briefs.

**Outputs:** page title, slug, outline, CTA, examples needed, schema suggestion.

**Prompt template:**

```text
Create a full content brief for Rhymes & Poetry.

Topic: {{TOPIC}}
Target reader: {{READER}}
Search intent: {{INTENT}}
Primary keyword: {{KEYWORD}}

Include:
- Suggested URL slug
- Meta title under 60 characters
- Meta description under 155 characters
- H1
- H2/H3 outline
- 5 original example sections
- FAQ section
- Internal links to add
- Product/lead magnet CTA
- Instagram post ideas based on the article

Avoid generic filler. Make the page useful, emotionally specific, and human.
```

### Agent 3: Drafting Agent

**Purpose:** write first drafts from approved briefs.

**Rules:**

- Do not publish raw AI output.
- Add original examples and human edits.
- Keep intros short.
- Prioritize emotional specificity.
- Include “make it your own” guidance.

**Prompt template:**

```text
Write a first draft for Rhymes & Poetry based on this brief.

Requirements:
- Helpful, emotionally intelligent, and easy to scan
- No generic motivational fluff
- Include original examples
- Include practical customization tips
- Include internal-link placeholders
- Include a soft CTA for {{CTA}}
- End with an FAQ

Brief:
{{BRIEF}}
```

### Agent 4: Brand Voice Editor

**Purpose:** make drafts sound more like the brand.

**Brand voice:**

- Poetic but plainspoken
- Warm, not corny
- Helpful, not academic
- Emotional but not melodramatic
- Clear enough for someone who is overwhelmed and just wants the right words

**Prompt template:**

```text
Edit this article in the Rhymes & Poetry brand voice.

Make it:
- More human
- More emotionally specific
- Less generic
- Easier to scan
- More useful for someone trying to write something meaningful

Keep SEO structure intact. Do not keyword-stuff.

Draft:
{{DRAFT}}
```

## 7. Recommended AI Agent Stack

### Simple Stack

Best for starting quickly.

- ChatGPT or Claude for planning/drafting/editing
- Google Sheets or Airtable for content calendar
- Zapier or Make for automations
- Notion or Google Docs for editorial workflow
- WordPress, Wix, Webflow, or Framer for site publishing
- Google Search Console for SEO performance
- GA4 or Plausible for analytics

### More Advanced Stack

Best if you want semi-automated workflows.

- Content database: Airtable
- Automation: Make or Zapier
- Draft generation: OpenAI / Claude API
- CMS: WordPress, Webflow, or headless CMS
- Image generation: Canva, Adobe Express, or generative image tooling
- Analytics: GA4, Search Console, Looker Studio
- Email: Beehiiv, ConvertKit, Mailchimp, or Substack
- Social scheduling: Buffer, Later, Metricool, or Meta Business Suite

### Suggested Workflow

```text
Keyword idea → SEO Research Agent → Content Brief Agent → Human approval → Drafting Agent → Brand Voice Editor → Human edit → CMS upload → Technical QA Agent → Publish → Instagram Repurposing Agent → Newsletter Agent → Performance review
```

## 13. AI Search / Generative Search Optimization

AI Overviews and other AI search experiences can change traffic patterns because users may get summarized answers before clicking. Some recent research suggests AI search summaries may reduce clicks to informational publishers, so Rhymes & Poetry should create pages worth visiting beyond a basic answer.

### Practical Strategy

- Build pages with original examples, templates, downloadable resources, and tools.
- Use clear definitions and concise answers near the top.
- Add FAQs that answer real questions.
- Add unique emotional examples that AI summaries cannot fully replace.
- Build brand recognition through Instagram and email so traffic is not fully dependent on search.
- Avoid manipulative “GEO” tactics that try to game AI outputs.
- Make the site crawlable and technically clean.
- Add first-hand brand voice and original poem examples.

### What AI Search Should Be Able to Understand

For every major page, make the answer obvious:

```text
What is this page about?
Who is it for?
What problem does it solve?
What examples or tools are included?
What should the reader do next?
```

## 23. First AI Agents to Build

Build these first, in order:

2. **Content Brief Agent**
